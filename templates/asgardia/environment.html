Environment

India's geographical position makes it highly vulnerable to various natural disasters. According to the National Disaster Management Authority, over 58.6% of the country’s land is prone to earthquakes, and more than 12% is at risk of floods. Nearly 5,700 km of India’s 7,516 km coastline faces threats from cyclones and tsunamis, while 68% of its cultivable land is prone to droughts. This makes disaster preparedness critically important.

The risks are further intensified by factors like changing demographics, socio-economic conditions, unplanned urbanization, development in high-risk areas, environmental degradation, climate change, and health crises like epidemics and pandemics. Together, these challenges threaten the country’s sustainable development and endanger countless lives and livelihoods.

we understand the profound impact that losing a home or a loved one can have on individuals and families. In the aftermath of natural disasters, many people find themselves not only grieving but also facing the harsh reality of displacement and loss. Our mission is to provide compassionate support and practical assistance to those affected, helping them navigate through this challenging time.

We provide assistance to families who have lost their homes, including temporary shelter, essential supplies like food and clothing, and access to medical care. Our team is dedicated to ensuring that individuals and families have their basic needs met while they begin the healing process.

We recognize that losing a loved one or a home can lead to emotional distress. As part of our holistic approach, Asgardia Foundation provides counseling and psychological support to help individuals cope with their grief and trauma. Our trained professionals are here to listen, guide, and assist in the healing journey, ensuring that emotional well-being is prioritized alongside physical recovery.

What We Do

Our focus on supporting children and their families, responds to humanitarian crises through its Disaster Response program. From the Kashmir earthquake in 2005 to Cyclone Fani in 2019 and the Assam floods in 2022, Asgardia Foundation has acted swiftly to address the immediate needs of those affected by disasters. Our approach not only provides emergency relief but also emphasizes sustainable recovery, assisting individuals in rebuilding their lives through education, healthcare, and livelihood support.

Asgardia Foundation has initiated crisis management during various calamities, including the Tsunami, the Kashmir earthquake, the Maharashtra floods, the Bihar floods, and the Uttarakhand floods. Through these efforts, we remain committed to making a meaningful difference in the lives of disaster-affected communities.
